PhotoArchive for iPhone, iPad, and iPod Touch

What does PhotoArchive do?

Easily Upload Camera Roll Photos & Videos to FTP & Google Drive.

Once you have configured the Settings, this is how you use PhotoArchive.

Step 1: Launch PhotoArchive.
Step 2: Swipe to the FTP or Google Drive page.
Step 3: Tap Start.
Step 4: Wait for your new photos & videos to be archived.

Your irreplaceable memories are now safe. It’s FAST!

You can tap the screen at any time to cancel the archive. PhotoArchive will finish the current upload.

You can put PhotoArchive into the background by pressing the Home or Sleep/Wake buttons during the archive. PhotoArchive will finish the current upload, and notify you.

Awesome Features

  1. Save Time - Stop manually backing up your device to iTunes. Are you still using a USB cable? Stop manually backing up your photos & videos by importing them into iPhoto. You may still import them into iPhoto, but you can do it when you have time, not when you have to - your photos & videos are already safe!

  2. Save Money - Use iCloud Backup to back up everything except your camera roll, so there is no need to manually back up your device to iTunes. Turn off the camera roll from iCloud Backup, and stay under the 5.0 GB free limit.

  3. Archives - Does not synchronise. Will not download photos or videos from FTP or Google Drive to your device.

  4. Never Deletes - Will never delete a photo or video from FTP or Google Drive, or from your device.

  5. Smart Archive - Remembers the previous archive, and will only upload new photos & videos, so it’s FAST!

  6. Smart Photo View - Shows you your photos & videos that are in danger of being lost forever (not archived), and the ones that are safe in the event that something happens to your device (archived).

  7. Original Format, Full Resolution and All Metadata - Uploads your photos & videos in their original format, at full resolution, with all metadata - information about your photos & videos, e.g. where you took them. This includes large video file sizes!

Archive to FTP

PhotoArchive archives to your NAS (Network Attached Storage) or Mac/PC, using FTP (File Transfer Protocol). Most NAS devices already support FTP, but if you are using a Mac/PC you will need an FTP server. Get an FTP server for Mac OS X, or Windows.

FTP should only be used on Wi-Fi with WPA2 security, with your storage on the same local network. Don’t do it over the Internet! Be security smart.

Archive to Google Drive

PhotoArchive archives to Google Drive. With Google Drive you can store 15 GB for FREE, 100 GB for USD $1.99 / month, or 1 TB for USD $9.99 / month - that’s 1024 GB!

PhotoArchive will request limited access to your Google Drive: (1) Access to read file metadata, not content. (2) Access to upload your photos & videos. PhotoArchive requests access to read your Google Drive file metadata so it knows what photos & videos from your device have already been archived. It’s called Smart Archive.
