PhotoArchive Version History

May 19, 2014

Version 1.5 – May 19, 2014

  1. [Super Fast!] – PhotoArchive is now super fast. Please note that the first time you load PhotoArchive after the update has been applied, PhotoArchive will have forgotten the previous archives. Simply tap Start to perform a Full Archive Check, and PhotoArchive will be back to normal for future archives.

  2. [Full Archive Check.] – If fully archived you can still tap Start to perform a Full Archive Check, which means PhotoArchive will double check that all of your camera roll photos & videos are really archived to the destination. Don’t worry, it’s FAST!

  3. [Local notification of incomplete archive.] â€“ Added archive type (FTP or Google Drive) text to notification description.

  4. [All Crashlytics reported crashes fixed.] WooHoo!

Version 1.4 – May 17, 2014

  1. [Local notification of incomplete archive.] – If an archive is in progress and you press the Home or Sleep/Wake buttons (or the system launches another app), then PhotoArchive will attempt to finish the current photo or video upload in the background, before cancelling the archive. Once the archive is cancelled PhotoArchive will create a local notification so that you know how many photos & videos are still not archived.

  2. [User interface bug fixes.]

Version 1.3.1 – May 3, 2014

Fixed user interface bugs introduced in version 1.3.

These bugs were not related to archive operation. Your photos & videos are being archived perfectly with all previous versions of PhotoArchive.

Version 1.3 – April 26, 2014

With PhotoArchive 1.3 it’s now even easier to save your irreplaceable memories to both your home and cloud storage (off site backup).

  1. [Archive to Google Drive.] – Archive your camera roll to your Google Drive. With Google Drive you can store 15 GB for FREE, 100 GB for USD $1.99 / month, or 1 TB for USD $9.99 / month – that’s 1024 GB!

    PhotoArchive will request limited access to your Google Drive: (1) Access to read file metadata, not content. (2) Access to upload your photos & videos. PhotoArchive requests access to read your Google Drive file metadata so it knows what photos & videos from your device have already been archived. It’s called Smart Archive.

  2. [Archive Options: Start Date.] – If you have uploaded your photos & videos to your cloud storage using a different method, you can tell PhotoArchive to only archive your photos & videos created after a specified date.

  3. [Confirm use of cellular data.] – PhotoArchive will ask you to confirm use of your cellular data when not on Wi-Fi.

Version 1.2 – March 21, 2014

  1. [Gracefully finish current upload.] – If an archive is in progress and you press the Home or Sleep/Wake buttons (or the system launches another app), then PhotoArchive will attempt to finish the current photo or video upload in the background, before cancelling the archive. The next time you open PhotoArchive it will show you an updated Smart Photo View.

  2. [Icons are cool.] – Added icons to the ‘PhotoArchive Needs Photos Permission’ view to show you where to go, to give PhotoArchive permission to access your Photos.

Version 1.1 – March 10, 2014

  1. iPad user interface!

  2. PhotoArchive now stops the system putting the device to sleep when an archive is in progress. This is great for your initial archive when you have lots of photos & videos that have never been archived.

  3. Settings: If you change the FTP Server or Account details, the FTP Folder is reset (cleared).

Version 1.0 – March 5, 2014

Initial release.
