Consistency

Just do it.

December 20, 2014

In January I paid RunKeeper to use the data I gave them the previous year, and give me insight. The numbers and charts were disappointing. This wasn’t insight. I wrote that what mattered was the experience. One year later and RunKeeper still charge you for insight.

I discovered that RunKeeper enables you to export your data. ‘Get your export on’ as RunKeeper says. I downloaded my activity data as CSV, and attempted to analyse it.

What do you do with lots of data? Add up the numbers of course. I wanted to compare the last 2 years. The numbers didn’t tell me anything interesting that I didn’t already know. More workouts means more duration and calories burned. Less cycling means less distance and climb.

It was time to visualise the data for 2014 with some charts. Maybe charts would give me insight. First I charted workout duration, with a linear trend line.

The trend line showed me that my workout time had increased during the year. This combined with the distribution of the data points showed me a picture of consistency over the year. A sudden realisation, I knew this already!

The only visual I had been looking at in the RunKeeper app during the year was the ‘workouts per week’ chart. I had been aiming for at least 3 workouts per week. If I fell behind I would workout more.

Next I charted workouts per week, with a monthly moving average trend line.

This chart showed me a picture of where my consistency changed. In February I went on a work trip to New Zealand and San Francisco. In April I had a few weeks holiday. Of course the data doesn’t tell the full story. I did a lot of walking in New Zealand and San Francisco which I didn’t record in RunKeeper.

With the coming wave of wearable devices we will be capturing more data about ourselves than ever before. This will create many possibilities, and many questions.

It’s clear that experience is critical, but so is consistency.

You don’t need to pay for insight.

Just do it.
